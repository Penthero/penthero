Chapter 4 - Level Up!

Link to comment

This is exactly how it works, only killing. Using skills levels up the skill itself, but to level up your class there is no shortcut, only killing things. Crafters are indeed a rare commodity, although they do not need to level up their class to have their main skill at a high level.

Most livestock is indeed level 1 or 2, giving very little xp, though the xp requirements for the first few levels are really low, so it adds up over time and a villager might reach about level 15 at some point just by virtue of killing chickens for food over their whole life. It's not that killing something of a lower level gives no experience, but it very rapidly gives increasingly less as the level gap widens. Keep in mind anything the narrator says when in Sofia's pov is still only really what Sofia experiences but doesn't actively think in phrases, she's sometimes straight up wrong or make false assumptions.

Either way, the reality of this system is that most humans won't even reach level 10 in their lifetime, by design. As for how anyone could possibly still get experience at higher levels without having to hunt that one wolf that decimated half the kingdom's sheeps for xp, or resorting to mass murder, it's a very simple case of not every species/race/individual being born at level 1.

I can't say much more about the why or how the system ended up like this without spoiling major plot points, so this will have to do for now (｀• ω •´)ｂ
